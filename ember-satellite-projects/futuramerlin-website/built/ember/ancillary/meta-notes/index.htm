Prerequisites for succeeding with Ember

  • Become more functional in life:
    1. Write a summary of the challenges that are getting in the way of my success in life, including: getting out of bed and taking action on stuff; this summary should include a checklist of things that I am trying to accomplish that indicates which are done, possible, or presently not practically doable for me.
    2. Until ready to return to school and do relevant paperwork:
      1. Try last new strategy and/or strategy given;
      2. Make list of strategies that I have tried and had not work for enacting a permanent remedy of these issues, highlighting ones that worked temporarily, and include list of people who have aided me in my pursuit of a resolution of these issues;
      3. Take list to the person to whom I was last referred and request new strategy/ies and/or referral(s).
  • Finish school, so that I have a degree, and can get a job and support myself financially, and use that degree to be respected by people (have them take my ideas seriously).
  • Learn to communicate with people in such a manner that they take me seriously, and don't reject my ideas without considering their merits or based on the consequences of my being neuroatypical.
  • Become friends with people who are interested in the Ember project, both because it is a complex project and will need assistance to complete, and because I will be much better able to function if I have friends. At least one of these people should be good at business and planning. Also because it's nice having friends, and it is hopefully nice for them too.
  • Learn how to express my plans without them sounding unrealistic, so that the people I must become friends with don't decide I'm too idealistic before I get them interested in the project.
  • Learn to: focus on what I'm working on, persistently work on it without getting distracted, and regulate my sleep schedule and my other lifestyle choices in a logical manner regardless of what I feel like doing at any given moment.
  • Learn to think about things in a manner that enables breaking them down into logical parts that I can realistically accomplish, even when the overarching project is enormous.
